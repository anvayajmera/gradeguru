Frequently Asked Questions

A GPA, or Grade Point Average, is a number that summarizes academic performance by averaging letter grades over a period of time.
Grade Guru consists of a simple GPA calculator.
To use it, just click on the buttons saying "GPA Calculator".
A weighted GPA accounts for the difficulty of classes by giving a numerical boost to more challenging courses, while an unweighted GPA treats all classes equally.
The letter grade "U" represents a numerical grade that is under a 65. It is quite similar to the letter grade "F".
The GPA calculations that Grade Guru uses are compatible with most institutions' grading systems.
However, the calculation system of Bridgewater-Raritan High School was specifically used.
We thoroughly verify our resources before we release them on Grade Guru. We prioritize accuracy and reliability.
Email gradegurubusiness@gmail.com with your name, age, the resource you want to provide, and a picture of a license for verification.
After creating an account, you can store the number of marking periods you had, and your weighted/unweighted GPA.
The date on each of the data boxes represents when you saved the data. This can come in handy when trying to know when you had a certain GPA.
We strive in making Grade Guru the best it can be using feedback!
